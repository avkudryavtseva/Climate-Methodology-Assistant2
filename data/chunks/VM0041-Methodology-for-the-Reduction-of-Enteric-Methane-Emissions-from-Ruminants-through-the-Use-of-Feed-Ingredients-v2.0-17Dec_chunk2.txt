This methodology provides procedures to estimate enteric methane (CH4) emission reductions 
generated from the suppression or inhibition of methanogenesis, achieved by the introduction 
of a feed ingredient into ruminant diets. This methodology considers emission reductions only 
from enteric fermentation. 
Feed ingredients applicable under this methodology reduce CH4 emissions by directly acting on 
the population of methanogenic archaea in the rumen, or by suppressing CH4 production 
through modification of the rumen environment, thus limiting methanogenesis. 
Depending on the location in which a project is implemented and the availability of data, this 
methodology provides three approaches to the quantification of baseline emissions and two 
approaches to the quantification of project emissions. Specifically, the quantification of 
baseline emissions may be performed using data from either on-site direct measurements, or 
by applying one of two different Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)-
recommended methods to model emissions using country-specific or peer-reviewed biometric
