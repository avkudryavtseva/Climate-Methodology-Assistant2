8.1 
Baseline Emissions 
Baseline emissions from existing ODS or HFC contained in products and/or stockpiles are 
determined with the following equation:  
𝐵𝐸𝑦= 𝐵𝐸𝑟𝑒𝑓𝑟,𝑦+ 𝐵𝐸𝑓𝑜𝑎𝑚,𝑦+ 𝐵𝐸𝑝𝑟𝑜𝑝,𝑦+ 𝐵𝐸𝑓𝑖𝑟𝑒,𝑦 
(1) 
Where:  
BEy 
= Total quantity of baseline emissions from refrigerants, blowing agents, 
propellants, and fire suppression agents from products and/or 
stockpiles that would be released into the atmosphere in year y in the 
absence of the project activity (t CO2e) 
BErefr,y 
= Baseline emissions from refrigerants that would be released into the 
atmosphere in year y in the absence of the project activity (t CO2e) 
BEfoam,y 
= Baseline emissions from blowing agents that would be released into 
the atmosphere in year y in the absence of the project activity (t CO2e) 
BEprop,y 
= Baseline emissions from propellants that would be released into the 
atmosphere in year y in the absence of the project activity (t CO2e)

= Baseline emissions from fire suppression agents that would be 
released into the atmosphere in year y in the absence of the project 
activity (t CO2e) 
 
This equation must use the adjusted baseline emission values based on the rate of compliance 
(CompRy) in accordance with the equations below to reflect gradually increasing compliance of 
the source country, where relevant.

consumption at the aggregation and destruction facilities, ODS or HFC transportation, and ODS 
or HFC destruction.

using project-specific data (Option B).

8.3 
Leakage 
Leakage emissions may occur where the refrigerant or fire suppression agent would have been 
reused in the baseline scenario and must be substituted by other chemicals in the project 
scenario, with gradual release. Where refrigerants and fire suppression agents are destroyed, 
their continued demand will lead to the consumption of other refrigerant and fire suppression 
agent chemicals whose production is still legal.  
Project activities where the baseline scenario is the atmospheric release of the substance or its 
continued storage in a stockpile (e.g., because it cannot be legally sold) are not required to 
account for this source of leakage. 
For project activities where the baseline scenario is recovery and reuse, the project proponent 
must consider a substitute chemical derived from official published data, research, industry 
studies (e.g., UNEP Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Technical Options Committee, UNEP Fire 
Suppression Technical Options Committee), or default values.
