9.3.1 Composition and Quantity Analysis Requirements for Concentrated Sources 
Prior to destruction, the project proponent must determine the precise mass and composition 
of refrigerants, blowing agents, fire suppression agents, and propellants to be destroyed.  
Mass must be determined by individually measuring the weight of each container of ODS 
and/or HFC:  
1) When it is full prior to destruction; and  
2) After it has been emptied and the contents have been fully purged and destroyed.  
The mass of ODS, HFC, and any contaminants is equal to the difference between the full and 
empty weight, as measured.  
9.3.1.1 Weighing Requirements 
The following requirements must be met when weighing containers of ODS and/or HFC:  
1) A single scale must be used for generating both full and empty weight tickets at the 
destruction facility.  
2) Weighing instruments must comply with relevant national requirements and/or 
international standards (e.g., EC Directive 2009/23/EC or International Organization for 
Legal Metrology OIML R 76-1). They must be regularly calibrated, as set out in relevant 
national requirements and/or international standards, to accuracy appropriate to the 
instrument’s accuracy class. The instruments must have a measuring range 
corresponding to the capacity of containers and tanks weighed. Scales found to be out 
of tolerance must be recalibrated.  
3) The full weight must be measured no more than two days prior to commencement of 
destruction per the Certificate of Destruction. 
4) The empty weight must be measured no more than two days after the conclusion of 
destruction per the Certificate of Destruction.

9.3.1.4 Composition and Quantity Analysis Requirements for Mixed ODS and/or HFC 
Containers holding mixed ODS and/or HFC (i.e. non-commercial blends. Marketable blends of 
refrigerants, e.g., R-410A are not considered mixed ODS or HFCs) must also be processed and 
measured for composition and concentration according to the requirements in this section. The 
sampling required under this section may be conducted before or after delivery to the 
destruction facility. The circulation and sampling activities must be directed and conducted by a 
third-party organization (i.e., not the project proponent) and by individuals who have been 
properly trained for the functions they perform. Circulation and sampling may be conducted at 
the project proponent’s facility. The project description must specify the procedures by which 
mixed ODS and/or HFC are analyzed.  
The composition and concentration of ODS and/or HFC on a mass basis must be determined 
for each container. The results of the composition analysis in Section 9.3.1.3 must be used by 
the validation/verification body (VVB) to confirm that the destroyed ODS and/or HFC is the 
same ODS and/or HFC that is sampled under the requirements in this section and that the 
mass of any non-eligible substances (e.g., hydrocarbons, water, high boiling residues) in the 
mixture has been subtracted from the total mass. Prior to sampling, the ODS and/or HFC 
mixture must be circulated in a container that meets all of the following criteria:

9.3.2.3 Analysis Requirements 
All samples must be analyzed using the heating method to extract ODS foam blowing agent 
from the foam samples, as described in Scheutz et al. (2007a) and summarized below: 
1) Each sample must be prepared to a thickness no greater than 1 cm, placed in a 1123 
mL glass bottle, weighed using a calibrated scale, and sealed with Teflon-coated septa 
and aluminum caps. 
2) To release the blowing agent from the foam, samples must be incubated in an oven for 
48 hours at 140 °C. 
3) When cooled to room temperature, gas samples must be redrawn from the headspace 
and analyzed using gas chromatography. 
4) The lids must be removed after analysis, and the headspace must be flushed with 
atmospheric air for approximately five minutes using a normal compressor. Afterwards, 
septa and caps must be replaced and the bottles subjected to a second 48-hour 
heating step to drive out the remaining blowing agent from the sampled foam. 
5) When cooled down to room temperature after the second heating step, gas samples 
must be redrawn from the headspace and analyzed using gas chromatography. 
9.3.3 Destruction Facility Requirements 
Destruction of ODS and/or HFC must occur at a facility that has a valid host country permit for 
ODS and/or HFC destruction and that meets the screening criteria for destruction technologies 
set out in the most recent version of the UNEP TEAP TFDT (reproduced in full in Appendix 3 
from TEAP Report of the Task Force on Destruction Technologies, Chapter 2 (2002)).  
Use of a TEAP-approved destruction technology meeting all applicable national and local 
standards is sufficient to demonstrate compliance.  
These screening criteria are discussed in detail in Appendix 3 and include destruction and 
removal efficiency (DRE), emissions of dioxins/furans, emissions of other pollutants, and 
technical capability.10
