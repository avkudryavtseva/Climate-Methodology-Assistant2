9.3 
Description of the Monitoring Plan 
All monitoring requirements of AMS-III.AV. and applied tools must be followed in addition to the 
following requirements: 
1) Both the baseline and project survey questionnaires must contain a survey question that 
allows the project proponent to capture any instances of interactive effects occurring 
between a safe drinking water system/device and improved thermal energy generation 
technologies in a residence or institution. The survey results related to this aspect must be 
applied proportionally across the population of households to determine the emission 
reductions.  
2) The project proponent must compile data on each distributed device that is derived from 
the total sales record, with project technologies differentiated by different project 
scenarios. These data must be differentiated into sections based on the results of the 
applicable monitoring studies for each project scenario, so that GHG emission reductions 
can be calculated appropriately, section by section. The information captured for each 
project device included in the project activity must include, at a minimum: 
a) Date of sale 
b) Geographic area of sale 
c) Model/type of project technology sold/distributed 
d) Name, telephone number (where available), and address of recipient 
e) Unique alphanumeric ID or GPS coordinates of each device that is sold/distributed
